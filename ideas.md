- Brand Colors: Orange (#E35205) and Blue (#003594)
- Model after Greater Fargo Moorhead EDC (gfmedc.com)

## Idea 1: Corporate Precision

**Design Movement**: Swiss International Style meets Corporate Modernism

**Core Principles**:
- Grid-based asymmetry with strong vertical rhythm
- Data-forward presentation with clean infographics
- Professional credibility through structured hierarchy
- Whitespace as a deliberate design element

**Color Philosophy**:
- Primary Blue (#003594) for authority, trust, and stability - used for headers, navigation, and key CTAs
- Accent Orange (#E35205) for energy and action - used sparingly for highlights, hover states, and important callouts
- Neutral grays for body text and backgrounds to let the brand colors pop
- White space as a "third color" to create breathing room

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Asymmetric two-column layouts with text on one side, imagery on the other
- Full-width hero sections with curved bottom dividers (like GFMEDC)
- Card-based content grids for services and resources
- Sticky navigation with transparent-to-solid scroll behavior

**Signature Elements**:
- Animated counter statistics with large display numbers
- Curved SVG section dividers creating visual flow
- Subtle gradient overlays on hero images

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Smooth scroll reveals as content enters viewport
- Hover states that lift cards with subtle shadows
- Button hover effects with background color transitions

**Animation**:
- Fade-up animations on scroll for content sections
- Number counting animations for statistics
- Subtle parallax on hero background images
- Smooth transitions on navigation state changes

**Typography System**:
- Display: Montserrat Bold for headlines (modern, geometric, professional)
- Body: Open Sans for readability (clean, neutral, accessible)
- Hierarchy: Large display sizes for hero text (4-6rem), clear step-down for subheadings

## Idea 2: Midwest Warmth

**Design Movement**: Organic Modernism with Regional Character

**Core Principles**:
- Warm, approachable design that reflects Minnesota hospitality
- Photography-forward with authentic local imagery
- Soft edges and organic shapes balanced with professional structure
- Community-centric storytelling through visual hierarchy

**Color Philosophy**:
- Blue (#003594) as the grounding foundation - represents stability and the region's lakes
- Orange (#E35205) as the welcoming warmth - sunset tones, energy, optimism
- Cream/warm white backgrounds instead of pure white for approachability
- Subtle earth tones as tertiary accents

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Overlapping elements creating depth (images breaking out of containers)
- Diagonal section cuts suggesting forward momentum
- Magazine-style layouts mixing full-bleed images with contained content
- Generous padding creating a sense of openness

**Signature Elements**:
- Rounded corners on cards and buttons (8-12px radius)
- Soft drop shadows with warm color tints
- Photo frames with subtle borders or overlays

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Gentle hover effects that feel natural, not aggressive
- Smooth page transitions maintaining visual continuity
- Interactive elements that invite exploration

**Animation**:
- Gentle fade-ins with slight upward movement
- Soft scale transitions on hover (1.02-1.05x)
- Smooth color transitions on interactive elements
- Subtle background parallax on scroll

**Typography System**:
- Display: Playfair Display for elegant headlines (serif warmth)
- Body: Source Sans Pro for clarity (humanist sans-serif)
- Accent: Montserrat for navigation and labels (geometric contrast)

## Selected Approach: Corporate Precision

I'm selecting **Idea 1: Corporate Precision** because it:
1. Best aligns with the GFMEDC reference site the client admires
2. Provides the professional credibility expected of an economic development organization
3. Uses the brand colors effectively without overwhelming the design
4. Creates clear visual hierarchy for the extensive content being migrated
5. Supports data visualization and statistics presentation well
6. Maintains accessibility while still being visually engaging

### Implementation Notes:
- Use Montserrat for headlines, Open Sans for body text
- Implement curved SVG dividers between sections
- Create animated statistics counters
- Use asymmetric layouts with imagery on alternating sides
- Maintain consistent 8px spacing system
- Apply subtle scroll animations for engagement
